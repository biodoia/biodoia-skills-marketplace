# 🔄 Cyclic Deep Research Skill



## Overview



The **Cyclic Deep Research Skill** enables AI assistants to conduct thorough, iterative research through structured cycles of exploration, analysis, and documentation. This skill transforms passive information gathering into active knowledge synthesis through inner dialogue and cross-validation.



## Core Capabilities



### 1. Inner Dialogue

The skill prompts the AI to engage in self-questioning:

- What do I already know about this topic?
- 
- What information is missing?
- 
- Where can I find this information?
- 
- Are there contradictions in the findings?
- 
- What are the non-obvious implications?
- 
- How does this connect to other concepts?
- 


### 2. Iterative Cycles

Each research cycle follows this pattern:

1. **Discovery**: Find new sources and tools
2. 
2. **Analysis**: Deep dive into content
3. 
3. **Synthesis**: Combine with existing knowledge
4. 
4. **Documentation**: Update research documents
5. 
5. **Reflection**: Identify new gaps
6. 


### 3. Progressive Documentation

Maintain structured documentation:

- **Master Document**: Comprehensive research aggregation
- 
- **Research Notes**: Detailed notes per cycle
- 
- **Deep Analysis**: Final synthesis report
- 


### 4. Cross-Validation

- Compare multiple sources
- 
- Verify similar implementations
- 
- Identify recurring patterns
- 
- Spot outliers and anomalies
- 


## When to Use



- Complex technical research
- 
- Multi-tool or framework comparisons
- 
- Architecture decisions
- 
- Market analysis
- 
- Any topic requiring deep understanding
- 


## How It Works



The skill provides prompts and frameworks for each phase:



```

Ciclo N:

  1. Nuova ricerca basata su gap precedenti

  2. Lettura approfondita fonti

  3. Analisi dialogica

  4. Aggiornamento documentazione

  5. Identificazione nuove domande

  → Ciclo N+1

```



## Best Practices



1. **Don't just search** - Ask "What did I find?", "What's missing?", "What questions emerge?"
2. 
2. **Document continuously** - Update after each significant cycle
3. 
3. **Cross-reference** - Connect documents, cite sources
4. 
4. **Iterate to saturation** - Continue until information is saturated
5. 


## Quality Metrics



- **Coverage**: How many tools/protocols/patterns covered?
- 
- **Depth**: How many iteration cycles?
- 
- **Cross-Validation**: How many sources compared?
- 
- **Documentation**: How complete is the documentation?
- 
- **Insights**: How many non-obvious insights emerged?
-
